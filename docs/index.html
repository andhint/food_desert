Buffalo Civic Innovation Challenge: Healthy Choices and Food Deserts in Buffalo

Food deserts are areas where quality, affordable food is limited. Generally, this means a lack of supermakets or grocery stores in area. These areas generally have convenience stores in the area which have less afforadble and offer limited or no whole foods.

The goal of our project was to display food deserts on a street by street basis. Food deserts have been mapped out by the USDA before, but only a county level. We believed that it was possible to view this on a much more granular level. There is no official definition of a food desert so we tried keep our definition as simple as possible. We divided the city into a number of small squares and determined how many options were within one mile of each square.

Data

We sourced our data from Open Street Maps (OSM). OSM has an API were all supermarkets/grocery stores/convenience store locations could be sourced. Since we're using OSM this same map can be replicated for any city by just changing the bounds when searching for stores. We used a dataset from Buffalo Open Data to trim the dataset to just Buffalo's boundaries for aesthetic purposes.
